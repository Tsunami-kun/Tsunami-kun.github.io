## Overview

**BimanGrasp** studies a simple gap: many daily objects are naturally handled with two hands, but large-scale grasp synthesis has mostly focused on one hand. The project builds a physics-based pipeline for generating stable bimanual grasps on arbitrary 3D objects and releases the resulting dataset.

## Method

The core problem is posed as stochastic optimization over two dexterous hands. A composite energy balances four requirements:

- stable force closure
- contact with the object surface
- low penetration, including hand-object and inter-hand collision
- kinematic feasibility under joint limits

Each candidate grasp is initialized around the object and refined with Langevin-style optimization. Final poses are then checked in simulation rather than accepted by geometry alone.

## Dataset and Results

The resulting **BimanGrasp-Dataset** contains more than **150K** verified grasps on **900** everyday objects. Every grasp is validated in Isaac Gym under randomized gravity rotations, which keeps the dataset grounded in physical success instead of appearance alone.

To speed up inference, we also train a diffusion model on the synthesized grasps. With light post-processing, it reaches **69.87%** success while remaining much faster than full optimization.

## Why It Matters

BimanGrasp provides a practical starting point for studying dexterous two-hand interaction at scale. It also shows that a large amount of useful structure can come from geometry and physics before imitation or language priors are introduced.
